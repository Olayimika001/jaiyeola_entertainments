3-Month Stage Performance Course Outline

Jaiyeola Acting Academy in conjunction with Fala Films Multimedia

Month 1: Foundations of Stage Performance & Acting Basics

Topics

  • What is stage performance?
  • Types of performance (Drama, Comedy, Spoken Word, Musical Stage)
  • Stage space and stage directions (upstage, downstage, blocking basics)
  • Confidence building and overcoming stage fear
  • Voice and body awareness
  • Understanding character
  • Emotional truth and realism
  • Facial expressions and body language
  • Movement on stage

Practical Exercises

  • Simple monologue delivery before a group
  • Character role-play and improvisation exercises
  • Basic stage movement and expression practice

Month 2: Voice, Speech & Script Performance

Topics

  • Voice projection techniques
  • Diction and articulation
  • Breathing control
  • Emotional expression through voice
  • Introduction to script reading
  • How to break down a script
  • Understanding dialogue meaning
  • Timing and rhythm in performance
  • Interaction with other actors
  • Blocking and stage movement in scenes

Practical Exercises

  • Read and perform short scripts with emotion
  • Partner and group scene performances
  • Script interpretation and stage movement practice

Month 3: Rehearsal, Stage Production & Live Performance

Topics

  • Rehearsal etiquette and stage discipline
  • Working with directors and fellow actors
  • Team coordination on stage
  • Costume, props, and stage awareness
  • Managing stage mistakes and recovery
  • Planning a stage play production
  • Set design and stage setup basics
  • Final rehearsals and performance polishing
  • Live performance execution
  • Audience engagement and feedback

Final Practical Project

Plan, rehearse, and perform a complete stage play before a live audience.

End Goal of the 3-Month Training

By the end of the training, students should be able to:

  • Perform confidently on stage.
  • Deliver scripts with emotion, clarity, and expression.
  • Develop believable characters and interact effectively with other actors.
  • Use proper voice projection, body language, and stage movement.
  • Work as a disciplined team in live productions.
  • Successfully participate in a complete stage performance from rehearsal to showtime.
